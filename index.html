Pitching Exercises


These are some excercises that I think would help you with your arm strength as well as accuracy. You can do these drills against a wall with a tennis ball or with a partner. Once you get used to the motions, it might be benificial to even borrow a softball from Nicole just for the drill part. These drills are a progression, so I would reccommend doing these in order to get the most out of it.

Cues to Remember

  1. Be Athletic, almost like you're a quarterback.
  2. Try to keep the angle between your bicep and forarm at 90 degrees or a little under.
  3. Don't worry about getting your arm up, it should automatically go up.
  4. Again, be ATHLETIC

  1. Kneeling Throws

    • Do 5 - 10 throws
    • Before each throw, make sure to see that the angle between your bicep and forearm is around 90 degrees.
    • Follow through with your throw

  2. Standing Throws

    • Do 5 - 10 throws
    • If you look closely when he takes out the ball, he does a row motion where the elbow retracts back. Emphasize this part.
    • Once you get the hang of this, try to be smooth and fluid with your motion. Your arm should not pause or stop.

  3. Walking Backwards Throws

    • Do 5 - 10 throws
    • This is similar to the previous drill, but walking backwards.
    • Try to keep on walking while you throw.
    • Emphasize being fluid and the 90 degree rowing motion.

  4. Walking Forward and Backwards Throws

    • Do 5 - 10 throws
    • This incorporates the previous excercise, but now we are adding in a throw going forward
    • Try to get some sort of rhythm when you throw. Remember be athletic. Think you're a quarter back.
    • Emphasize being fluid and the 90 degree rowing motion.

  5. Sideways Swing Throws

    • Do 5 throws.
    • Keeping that 90 degree angle becomes important when doing this drill.
    • Try to be fluid and get a good rhythm
    • When doing your arm swings, make sure your glove arm is going up and out too.

  6. Stationary Sideways Throws

    • Do 5 -10 throws.
    • Keeping that 90 degree angle becomes important when doing this drill.
    • Try to be fluid and get a good rhythm
    • When doing your arm swings, make sure your glove arm is going up and out too.
